# Contributor Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want the TagoIO JavaScript SDK to be a respectful, productive place for contributors, maintainers, and users. We commit to keeping
participation open and harassment-free, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and
expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity, or other protected
status.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that helps the project:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Respecting different viewpoints and experience levels.
- Giving constructive feedback focused on the work.
- Accepting responsibility, apologizing when needed, and correcting behavior.
- Prioritizing what is best for the project and its users.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances.
- Trolling, insults, personal attacks, or political baiting.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing another person's private information without explicit permission.
- Conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in project spaces and when someone represents the project in public spaces. Examples include
GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, community forums, events, and social media posts tied to the project.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing the standards of acceptable behavior. They may remove, edit, or
reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct. They may
also temporarily or permanently ban contributors for behavior they consider inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or
harmful.

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior can be reported privately through the repository's
security or contact channels. Maintainers will review reports promptly and fairly, respect reporter privacy, and respond
in a way that protects the project and its participants.

## Enforcement guidelines

Maintainers may use the following responses depending on severity and history:

- Correction: private or public clarification of why behavior was inappropriate.
- Warning: a formal warning with consequences for continued behavior.
- Temporary ban: temporary loss of participation privileges.
- Permanent ban: permanent removal from project spaces.
